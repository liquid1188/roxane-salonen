About Roxane

Would it surprise you to hear I lead a double — even triple — life?

It’s true. The photos here hint at one of my faces — author, writer, speaker, and radio host. That’s the more polished version of me. I love being her. But when I’m not doing those things, you’ll find me kicking back — or running crazy — with my favorite people, the Salonen Seven. In that role I answer to Mom, Rox, Rock, and, when Troy is teasing, “Roseanne.”

My father named me after the heroine of Cyrano de Bergerac, to match our French surname, Beauclair — “clear and bright.” I grew up on the Fort Peck Reservation in northeastern Montana, where my parents taught school, before our family’s return to the Peace Garden State. After college in Moorhead, Minnesota, I married a man with a Finnish name — Salonen — and kept the B. for Beauclair, because it’s still part of me, and always will be.

Music is another petal that clings to my center. Now and then I cantor for the early Mass with a pianist to guide me — singing the word of God is a pretty special thing, and I’m happy to do it. And when a deadline looms and the house grows loud, I slip away to the quiet of a cloistered monastery, where the stillness is good for mind, body, and soul.

Each identity I step into adds a petal to the whole flower. But of all the roles I find myself in, the one at the very center — the one that started it all, and goes deepest — is daughter of God. It is this that brings meaning to all the rest.

Wife, and mother to the Salonen Seven.
